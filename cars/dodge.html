Dodge Challanger SRT Hellcat 2019

  • Engine: 6.2-liter V8
  • Horse Power: 707 hp
  • Torque: 650 ft-lb
  • 0-60 MPH: 3.6 seconds

The 2019 Dodge Challenger SRT Hellcat is a high-performance sports car that was built by the Dodge division of FCA US LLC. It is powered by a supercharged 6.2-liter V8 engine that produces 707 horsepower and 650 lb-ft of torque.

It comes standard with a 6-speed manual transmission, but an 8-speed automatic is also available. It has a top speed of 199 mph and can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 3.6 seconds. It also features a unique design with bold exterior styling, including a large front grille, a hood scoop, and a rear spoiler.

Inside, the Hellcat offers comfortable seating for up to five passengers, as well as a variety of technology and convenience features. Overall, it is a powerful and iconic muscle car that is sure to turn heads on the road.

Dodge Charger 2015

  • Engine: 5.7-liter V8
  • Horse Power: 370 hp
  • Torque: 395 lb-ft
  • 0-60 MPH: 5.1 seconds

The 2015 Dodge Charger is a full-size sedan that was available with a variety of engine options, including a 3.6-liter V6 and a 5.7-liter V8. It featured a redesigned exterior, an updated interior, and new technology such as the Uconnect infotainment system.

The 2015 Charger also had a variety of trim levels available, including the SE, SXT, R/T, R/T Road & Track, R/T Scat Pack, and SRT 392.

Dodge Challanger 1973

  • Engine: 5.6-liter V8
  • Horse Power: 335 hp
  • Torque: 410 lb-ft
  • 0-60 MPH: 7.5 seconds

The 1973 Dodge Challenger was a pony car produced by the Dodge division of Chrysler. It was available as a two-door coupe or convertible and was offered in a variety of trim levels, including the base model, Rallye, and R/T. The Challenger shared its E-body platform with the Plymouth Barracuda.

In 1973, the Challenger was available with a variety of engines, including a 225-cubic-inch (3.7 L) slant-six, a 318-cubic-inch (5.2 L) V8, a 340-cubic-inch (5.6 L) V8, and a 383-cubic-inch (6.3 L) V8. The R/T version was powered by a standard 335 horsepower 400 cid V8 engine.

The 1973 model year was the final year for the first generation Challenger, which was in production from 1970 to 1974.
