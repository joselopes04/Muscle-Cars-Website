Pontiac Firebird 1967

  • Engine: 5.3 L V8
  • Horse Power: 350 hp
  • Torque: 442 ft-lb
  • 0-60 MPH: 7.5 seconds

The 1967 Pontiac Firebird was a compact car produced by Pontiac as a response to the Ford Mustang. It was the first model year of the Firebird, and it shared its F-body platform with the Pontiac Tempest.

The 1967 Firebird was available in two body styles, the sporty coupe and the luxurious convertible. It was offered in two trim levels: the base model and the sporty Firebird Sprint.

The 1967 Firebird was powered by a variety of engines, including a 230 cubic inches (3.8 L) inline six-cylinder engine, and a 326 cubic inches (5.3 L) V8 engine. The Firebird Sprint was powered by a standard 215 horsepower OHC 6-cylinder and a optional 326 cubic inches (5.3 L) V8 engine rated at 350 horsepower and 442 lb-ft of torque. The car had a 0-60 mph acceleration time of around 7.5 seconds.

Pontiac Firebird Trans AM 1988

  • Engine: 5.0-liter V8
  • Horse Power: 205 hp
  • Torque: 300 lb-ft
  • 0-60 MPH: 7.5 seconds

The 1988 Pontiac Firebird Trans Am was a sports car produced by Pontiac as a high-performance version of the Firebird. The Trans Am was available as a coupe or convertible, and it was offered in a variety of trim levels, including the base model, Trans Am, and GTA.

The 1988 Trans Am was powered by a 5.0-liter V8 engine that produced 205 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque. It featured a four-speed automatic or five-speed manual transmission. The car had a 0-60 mph acceleration time of around 7.5 seconds.

The 1988 Trans Am featured a sport-tuned suspension, four-wheel disc brakes, and a variety of cosmetic upgrades such as Trans Am badges and emblems, special wheel covers, and a blacked-out grille. It also had a variety of advanced features for the time, such as power windows, power locks, and air conditioning.
