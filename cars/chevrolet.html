Chevrolet Chevelle Malibu SS 1970

  • Engine: L78 396ci V8
  • Horse Power: 375 hp
  • Torque: 415 ft-lb
  • 0-60 MPH: 6.5 seconds

The 1970 Chevrolet Chevelle Malibu SS was a muscle car produced by Chevrolet as a high-performance version of the Chevelle Malibu. The SS (Super Sport) package was available on both the coupe and convertible body styles.

The 1970 Chevelle Malibu SS was available with a variety of engines, including the standard 300 horsepower V8, the L65 327ci/250 horsepower and a optional 375 horsepower L78 396ci V8 engine. The L78 engine was rated at 375 horsepower and 415 lb-ft of torque.

The car had a 0-60 mph acceleration time of around 6.5 seconds. The 1970 Chevelle Malibu SS featured a sport-tuned suspension, front disc brakes, and a variety of cosmetic upgrades such as SS badges and emblems, special wheel covers, and a blacked-out grille.

Chevrolet Camaro 2019

  • Engine: 6.2-liter V8
  • Horse Power: 455 hp
  • Torque: 455 lb-ft
  • 0-60 MPH: 4 seconds

The 2019 Chevrolet Camaro is a sports car produced by Chevrolet. It was available in a variety of trim levels, including the LS, LT, SS, and ZL1. The Camaro features a new design and new technology, including the latest version of the Chevrolet Infotainment System.

The 2019 Camaro SS was equipped with a 6.2-liter V8 engine that produced 455 horsepower and 455 lb-ft of torque. It could accelerate from 0-60 mph in around 4 seconds. The SS model also featured a sport-tuned suspension, Brembo brakes, and a variety of cosmetic upgrades such as a unique front grille, rear spoiler, and 20-inch wheels.

In addition to the V8 engine, the 2019 Camaro also offered a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine and a 3.6-liter V6 engine.

Chevrolet Corvette 1982

  • Engine: L83 5.7-liter V8
  • Horse Power: 230 hp
  • Torque: 300 lb-ft
  • 0-60 MPH: 6.3 seconds

The 1982 Chevrolet Corvette was a sports car produced by Chevrolet. It was the third generation of the Corvette, which was in production from 1968 to 1982.

The 1982 Corvette was available with two engine options: a base model with a 200 horsepower, 5.7-liter V8 and a optional engine, a 230 horsepower, 4-barrel carbureted L83 5.7-liter V8. The L83 engine had torque of 300 lb-ft.

The 1982 Corvette had a 0-60 mph acceleration time of around 6.5 seconds for the base model and 6.3 seconds for the L83 engine option.

The 1982 Corvette featured a new design that included a more aerodynamic shape, a new interior, and new technology such as electronic fuel injection and a digital instrument cluster. The car also had an improved suspension, which provided better handling and ride comfort.
